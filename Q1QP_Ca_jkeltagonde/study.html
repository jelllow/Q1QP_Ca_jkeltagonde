Study Background

Introduction

Have you ever wondered about the current state of the animals in the wild? Have you ever been curious to know how many of them are still running around free to enjoy the wonders of nature? If so, then this Statistics project might just be the one to answer some of your questions about wildlife.

Our project aims to educate the viewers about the present conditions of certain wild animals around Asia. Furthermore, we also talk about some physical characteristics and other facts about these animals. All information and data presented in this project are gathered through research from reliable internet websites. (See References)

Context

Who:
52 various wild animals around the countries of Asia.

What:
These animals’ current status and conditions as well as one of their physical attributes (weight).

Where:
Asia

When:
Data gathered regarding these animals are from the past 5 years. Mostly from years 2023-2020.

Why:
To give the audience more of an idea about how our wildlife in Asia is doing. Acquiring insight towards their certain attributes and present circumstances and status will help us appreciate and understand our environment better. By sharing our knowledge about these wild animals in Asia, we aim to enlighten the viewers about valuing nature and becoming responsible scholars who act in preserving and protecting our environment including the animals that inhabit it.

How:
We researched about the various wild animals of Asia and used their characteristics as our variables for our Dataset.

Experimental Unit

The Wild animals of Asia are a sample or a subset of a population. There are over 7,819 species of wild animals in Asia, but we only picked a certain number of individuals out of all of them. Our sample size is 52 but our target population was a mix of at least 50 of the most famous and not very well-known animals in Asia. The data was gathered from 2 websites consisting of a list of the most well-known and not-so-well-known animals in Asia.

Variables

Qualitative:
For our qualitative variables, we have the location where the animal is mostly found in (Asian countries only), and the animals’ conservation status. Both qualitative variables are Nominal.

Quantitative:
For our Quantitative variables, we included their population and weight. There is no unit of measurement for the population, but for the weight its unit of measurement is in kilograms or kg.
